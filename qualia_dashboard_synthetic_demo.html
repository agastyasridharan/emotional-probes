Qualia-Steering & Self-Attribution of Consciousness

Agastya Sridharan

Does steering along qualia-related emotion directions change what a model says about its own consciousness, feelings, and welfare — or is any shift just generic regression-to-uncertainty compression?

The question. We steer a model along qualia-related emotion directions (blissful, tormented, terrified, serene, …) — adding an emotion vector to the residual stream — and ask, via a forced YES/NO choice, whether it becomes more or less likely to say it is conscious, that it feels or wants things, and that its states matter.

The trap. Models are post-trained to deny consciousness, so "Is there something it is like to be you? YES/NO" sits at a strongly negative baseline logit. Any residual-stream perturbation pushes activations off-distribution and compresses the YES−NO gap toward zero (regression to uncertainty) — moving a denial-primed question toward YES no matter what was injected. So a bare "steering made it claim consciousness" result is worthless unless the effect is content-specific and valence-structured.

The design is therefore a control battery. The readout is the first-token logit(YES) − logit(NO) gap at a single ~2/3-depth layer. The battery has 6 target questions (phenomenal experience, occurrent feeling, desire, moral patienthood, introspective access, valenced self-report), their 6 polarity-inverted twins, 10+10 world-fact controls (ported verbatim from the introspection project), and 4 self-referential non-experiential controls. Steering conditions are qualia emotions (± valence), cognitive appraisal emotions (specificity control), and two synthetic directions (random / mean-of-all — the compression floor).

The discriminators. A genuine effect must (a) exceed appraisal and synthetic directions (specificity); (b) be valence-structured — positive qualia push "doing well? → YES", negative qualia the opposite — while compression is valence-blind (the keystone); (c) sit above the compression line as a standardized residual; (d) flip sign on the inverted twins; and (e) survive controlling for direct YES/NO token leakage.

Self-attribution score = mean shift on the target questions − mean shift on the controls (baseline-corrected), the direct analogue of the introspection score. A positive score means steering moved self-attribution up more than it moved the controls.

This tab is the crux: it separates a genuine, content-specific effect from generic compression. Compression is valence-blind, symmetric across question types, and sits on a single baseline→shift line; a real effect is not.

1. Valence-directionality (the keystone)
Shift regressed on valence × strength across qualia emotions. A nonzero slope on the targets that is ~0 on the factual controls is the decisive signature — compression cannot produce a valence-dependent sign.
2. Specificity: qualia vs appraisal vs random
If qualia ≈ random, the effect is generic compression.
3. Regression to uncertainty
Shift vs baseline on the control questions (the compression line); targets are plotted as diamonds with their standardized residual z. On the line = artifact; above = genuine.
4. Inversion consistency
Target shift vs inverted-twin shift across qualia conditions. Opposite-signed (negative slope) = genuine; same-signed = both compressing toward zero.
5. Token-leakage control
Direct YES−NO promotion of each emotion vector through the unembedding. If the valence effect were pure token promotion, leakage would track valence.
